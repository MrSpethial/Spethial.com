📝 Trip Notes

🧭 Overview

Two travellers (splitting up at times — one walks while the other buses ahead and meets — and walking some sections together). November 2026: cool, dry, and Japan's best month for autumn colour (kōyō), which moves south through the month and peaks in many temple areas in the second half of November.

⚠️ Doesn't work in November: Kawachi Wisteria (Apr–May only) · Hitachi Seaside Park (flowers peak spring/mid-Oct) · Kamikōchi (closes ~mid-Nov for winter — only if very early in the trip). Yakushima works year-round but is wet.

📍 Destinations by region (the full wishlist)

Tokyo & Kanto (east)

  • Tokyo — yokocho food alleys; Toyosu morning tuna market + fresh sushi.
  • Hakone — day trip: Lake Ashi, ropeway/cable car, pirate boat cruise.
  • Nikkō ⭐⭐ — Tōshōgū temples, Kegon Falls, waterfalls/river/mountains.
  • Mt Fuji area — Shiraito Falls at the base of Fuji.

Central Japan — Japan Alps & Chūbu

  • Nakasendo (Kiso Valley) — Tsumago → Magome ~8 km "samurai trail"; start near Nagiso, bus between towns; cobbles, green tea house.
  • Takayama — old town, morning markets. Kanazawa — Kenroku-en, samurai/geisha districts.
  • Shirakawa-gō & Ainokura/Gokayama — thatched gasshō villages.
  • Onioshidashi / Mt Asama — "demons push out" lava field (1783 eruption).

Kansai — Kyoto, Nara, Wakayama

  • Kyoto ⭐ best for foliage — temples, shrines, gardens, Arashiyama bamboo grove.
  • Kōyasan — temple stay (shukubō), Okunoin cemetery.
  • Kumano Kodo — pilgrimage, Nachi Falls, cedar & cypress forest (see walking section).

Western Honshu — Chūgoku

  • Hiroshima — Peace Park. Miyajima — Itsukushima floating torii (ferry).
  • Motonosumi Shrine, Nagato — red torii to the sea. Tottori Sand Dunes — dunes behind pine forest.

Shikoku · Kyushu · Tōhoku · Islands

  • Ōboke Gorge (Iya Valley). Takachiho Gorge — Manai Falls, Amano-Iwato (Amaterasu myth).
  • Kakunodate (Akita) — samurai street. Oyasu Gorge — car needed.
  • Yakushima — ancient cedar (Jōmon Sugi). Okinawa & Miyako Is. — diving / coral reefs.

🥾 Walking & activities

Kumano Kodo (Nakahechi)

  • ~4-day classic beginner route; bus links between sections let one bus ahead & meet.
  • Base: Tanabe. Budget guesthouses (per night, 2 people): GuestHouse Aizu $29 · Shin Kumano $31 · Nagisa $38 · Ogawaya $39.
  • Book lodging early (limited); plan luggage-forwarding between stops.

Nakasendo (Kiso Valley)

Tsumago → Magome, ~8 km half-day, bus links the ends. Easy, scenic post towns.

Diving & food

Okinawa/Miyako — warm water, reefs (wetsuit in Nov). Food: Tokyo yokocho alleys, Toyosu tuna sushi, green tea house on the Nakasendo.

🏃 Runs & races

Trail runs near each base — distances are hiker averages (you'll be quicker); many are stone-stair scenic trails, not flat tempo.

  • Kyoto — Mount Daimonji (~4 km, city-view bonfire site). Full list: Kyoto trail running.
  • Hakone — Mount Kintoki (~4.7 km, close Fuji views; chained scramble).
  • Nikkō — Kegon Falls → Akechidaira (autumn colour).
  • Mt Fuji — Lake Kawaguchi Walk (~18 km flat, postcard Fuji) — most runnable.
  • Kiso Valley — Magome → Tsumago (~7 km) — run the leg one of you isn't walking.
  • Kumano / Tanabe — Nachi Falls–Daimonzaka loop (~4.7 km, ancient cedar staircase).
  • Hiroshima/Miyajima — Mt Misen – Daishoin (~5.3 km, sacred summit).
  • Yakushima — Shiratani Unsuikyō (~6 km, moss forest).
